What Is My IP?

Quick answer: your public IP is the address the internet sees for your device/network. You can check it instantly on our homepage — we’ll also show your IPv4/IPv6, city/country, ISP and ASN with no logs.

Public vs local IP (and why both matter)

  • Public IP — visible on the internet, assigned by your ISP/CGNAT or VPN. This is what websites see.
  • Local IP — private address on your home/office Wi-Fi (e.g., 192.168.1.23). Not routable on the internet.

How to see my IP right now

Open the Check IP page. You’ll see:

  • IPv4/IPv6 (both if available)
  • Location (city/country)
  • Provider / ASN (your ISP or VPN network)
  • User-Agent (browser & OS)

How to find your local IP on each OS

Windows

cmd → ipconfig
Ethernet adapter Wi-Fi:
   IPv4 Address. . . . . . . . . . . : 192.168.1.23

macOS / Linux

# macOS
ifconfig | grep "inet " 
# Linux
ip addr show | grep "inet "

iOS / Android

iOS: Settings → Wi-Fi → (i) → IPv4 Address.
Android: Settings → Network & Internet → Wi-Fi → the connected network.

Why your IP location can look “wrong”

IP location relies on databases and the network path. With VPNs, mobile networks, or CG-NAT, the city can differ. See Why does my IP show the wrong location?

FAQ

Is my IP personal data? In many jurisdictions it can be, depending on context. We don’t store IPs or set tracking cookies.

Do I have both IPv4 and IPv6? Many networks provide dual-stack; our homepage shows both if available.

Can websites identify me by IP? IP alone identifies your network, not you. Combining IP with other signals can increase fingerprinting accuracy.
