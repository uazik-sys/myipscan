How to Check Your IPv6 Address and Compatibility

Updated: October 15, 2025

IPv6 is the next-generation Internet Protocol, designed to replace IPv4 and provide more addresses, better performance, and enhanced privacy. Checking your IPv6 address and compatibility helps you confirm whether your device, network, and ISP fully support the new standard. Here’s how to find your IPv6 address on any device — and test if it’s working properly.

1. How to find your IPv6 address

Windows

  1. Press Win + R, type cmd, and press Enter.
  2. In Command Prompt, type ipconfig and press Enter.
  3. Look for “IPv6 Address” under your active adapter.

macOS

  1. Open System Settings › Network.
  2. Select your active interface (Wi-Fi or Ethernet).
  3. Your IPv6 address appears under “Details” or “Advanced › TCP/IP”.
  4. Or use Terminal: ifconfig | grep inet6.

Android

  1. Go to Settings › Network & Internet › Wi‑Fi.
  2. Tap your connected Wi‑Fi network.
  3. Scroll down — IPv6 address appears in the “Advanced” section.

iPhone / iPad (iOS)

  1. Open Settings › Wi‑Fi.
  2. Tap the “ⓘ” next to your network.
  3. Scroll to the IPv6 section to see your address.

Linux

  1. Open Terminal.
  2. Type ip -6 addr show or ifconfig.
  3. Find your active interface (e.g., eth0 or wlan0) and check the “inet6” line.

2. How to check IPv6 compatibility and connectivity

  • Visit What is My IP — it shows both IPv4 and IPv6 if available.
  • Try test-ipv6.com to measure end-to-end IPv6 reachability.
  • Run DNS Lookup to confirm AAAA (IPv6) records resolve for websites.
  • If you only see IPv4 results, your ISP or router may not support IPv6 yet.

3. How to enable IPv6

  1. Ensure your router has IPv6 enabled — look under “Internet Settings › IPv6” or similar.
  2. Set connection type to “Auto (DHCPv6)” or “SLAAC”.
  3. Check your ISP’s support — some ISPs require specific modems or firmware updates.
  4. Restart your router and device after enabling.

4. Privacy extensions & dynamic IPv6

Modern systems use IPv6 privacy extensions to randomize interface identifiers. This helps prevent tracking based on a fixed IPv6. Each session may show a slightly different temporary address — this is normal and increases privacy.

5. Troubleshooting IPv6 issues

  • If IPv6 doesn’t appear, verify router support or firmware updates.
  • Check that firewall rules allow ICMPv6 (required for discovery).
  • Ensure your OS isn’t set to “Prefer IPv4”.
  • If on mobile data, note that some carriers disable IPv6 on tethered connections.
