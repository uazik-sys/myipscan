How to Find Your Local IP Address on Any Device

Updated: October 15, 2025

Your local IP address identifies your device inside your home or office network — it’s different from your public IP that the internet sees. Knowing your local IP helps you troubleshoot connections, set up printers or servers, or manage your router. Here’s how to find it on any device, plus a few security tips.

Windows

  1. Press Win + R, type cmd, and hit Enter.
  2. In Command Prompt, type ipconfig and press Enter.
  3. Look for “IPv4 Address” — that’s your local IP (e.g., 192.168.1.101).

macOS

  1. Open System Settings › Network.
  2. Select your active connection (Wi-Fi or Ethernet).
  3. Your local IP appears under “Status” or “Details”.
  4. Alternatively, open Terminal and type ifconfig | grep inet.

Android

  1. Go to Settings › Network & Internet › Wi‑Fi.
  2. Tap the connected Wi‑Fi network.
  3. Scroll to “Advanced” or “IP address”.

iPhone / iPad (iOS)

  1. Open Settings › Wi‑Fi.
  2. Tap the “ⓘ” icon next to your network.
  3. Find “IP Address” under the IPv4 section.

Linux

  1. Open Terminal.
  2. Type ip addr show or ifconfig.
  3. Find your active interface (e.g., eth0, wlan0) and read the “inet” address line.

Router (to see all connected devices)

  1. Type your router’s address (often 192.168.1.1 or 192.168.0.1) in a browser.
  2. Login with admin credentials.
  3. Open “Connected devices” or “DHCP clients” — you’ll see local IPs for every device.

Local vs public IP: what’s the difference?

Your local IP only works inside your network. It’s usually from one of these private ranges:

  • 192.168.0.0 – 192.168.255.255
  • 10.0.0.0 – 10.255.255.255
  • 172.16.0.0 – 172.31.255.255

Your public IP is assigned by your ISP and seen by websites and online services. You can check it with What is My IP.

Security and privacy tips

  • Local IPs aren’t exposed publicly, but devices on your LAN can still communicate — secure your Wi‑Fi with WPA2/3 and strong passwords.
  • Disable guest networks or restrict them to internet-only access.
  • Update router firmware regularly.
  • For external privacy, use a VPN to hide your public IP.
